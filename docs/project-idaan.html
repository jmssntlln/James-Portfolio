Portfolio Project

i.Daan

AI-driven complaint management for DPWH Lipa

Back to projects

Case Study

Transforming infrastructure reporting

Project Overview

  • Project Type Product Design · UX/UI · Front-End Development
  • Client DPWH Lipa
  • Project Year 2024 — 2025

Overview

i.Daan modernizes DPWH Lipa's complaint process by replacing walk-ins and phone calls with an AI-powered reporting and mapping system. Citizens can file complaints through a conversational chatbot, upload photos, and track issues on a live map. Employees verify, categorize, and resolve complaints through a web dashboard.

The Problem

The existing process was slow, manual, and fragmented:

  • 45-minute filing times
  • No way for citizens to track their complaint
  • Duplicate reports
  • Zero geospatial visibility
  • Heavy workload on staff

Users needed a faster, transparent, and centralized system.

Users

  • Citizens: Want fast reporting & visibility
  • DPWH Staff: Need easier validation
  • District Engineer: Needs analytics

UX Research Highlights

User insights that guided the structure of the chatbot, map interactions, and dashboard workflows:

  • Citizens found the current process "too slow and inconvenient."
  • Users frequently submitted duplicate reports due to lack of visibility.
  • Staff had difficulty prioritizing cases without proper categorization.
  • Both groups needed real-time updates to avoid delays and miscommunication.

Solution Summary

i.Daan delivers a unified end-to-end reporting system with an AI-powered mobile chatbot and a web dashboard for DPWH employees. The app streamlines complaint filing, automates categorization, and visualizes reports on a real-time Mapbox-powered map. Citizens get transparency and faster updates, while employees gain a structured validation workflow, analytics, and overdue monitoring tools.

Project Learnings

Reflection

Through this project, I realized how essential collaboration and user involvement are in building an effective system. Working with both citizens and DPWH employees allowed me to understand two very different perspectives, which shaped the overall design of the chatbot, map, and dashboard.

One major challenge was simplifying a process that was historically slow, manual, and full of steps. Turning that into a 1–3 minute digital workflow required continuous refinement and testing. This taught me to always re-evaluate assumptions and redesign based on real user behavior.

Another important learning was the value of measurable outcomes. Seeing that the system reduced processing time by up to 97% and achieved 89%–93% satisfaction from users validated the importance of thoughtful UX decisions. It proved that design is not just about visual appeal—it directly affects efficiency, transparency, and public trust.

This project strengthened my skills not only in interface design but also in research, prototyping, and working with real stakeholders. It showed me how technology and UX can truly simplify government processes and make public services more accessible.
